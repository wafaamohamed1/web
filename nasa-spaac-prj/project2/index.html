Partial Eclipse Begins

This is the initial stage of a solar eclipse. The Moon starts to move between the Sun and Earth, gradually blocking a portion of the Sun's disk. At this stage.

Total Eclipse Begins

As the Moon continues its path across the Sun, it eventually covers the entire disk of the Sun. The sky darkens considerably, and the Sun's corona becomes visible as a faint, glowing ring around the Moon.

Totality

The Moon completely obscures the Sun, and only the outer edges of the Sun's atmosphere, called the corona, are visible.

Total Eclipse Ends

After a period of totality, the Moon starts to move away from the Sun, gradually revealing more of the Sun's surface. This marks the end of the total solar eclipse.

Partial Eclipse Continues

As the Moon moves further away from the Sun, the partial eclipse resumes. The Moon gradually uncovers more of the Sun's disk, and the sky brightens again..
